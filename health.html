Vets!

Going to the vets for rabbits can be very stressful so you must make it as calm as you can for them you can do this by making the journey as gentle as you can for them talking to them in a calm voice and letting them know that there is nothing to be frightened of also by lining their crate with hay can make them feel more relaxed like they're at home. Also, when you get to the vets covering their crate with a blanket so your rabbit doesn't get scared of its surroundings and other pets. Your rabbit will need vaccinating every year to prevent diseases and to keep them well and healthy. Rabbit's claws should be about ½ cm (1/4 in) long. Rabbits front teeth grow all the time so you should give them a wooden block to gnaw on to prevent them growing strange. If their teeth get too long you need to ask a vet to shorten them or they'll grow at a strange angle.

Health checks:

  1. Have trouble breathing
  2. Not eating or drinking much
  3. Eyes, nose or ears running
  4. Limping
  5. Dirty bottom
  6. Dry skin or sore sliots esliecially around neck
  7. Scratching
  8. No cuts lumlis or bald liatches
  9. Not too fat or thin
  10. Firm back
